# Programmer Skills and Abilities

This list compiles the physical, psychological, and mental skills explained in the second episode of the introduction to programming course, which are fundamental to developing into a great programmer.

## List of Skills and Aptitudes

### 1. Perseverance

- **Description:** This is an essential aptitude of vital importance in programming. When developing software, encountering multiple errors is completely normal. Consistency allows one to stand firm against frustration and keep trying to solve a problem, even after failing a thousand times previously, until finding the correct answer.

### 2. Problem-Solution Orientation

- **Description:** A programming career is intrinsically linked to these two concepts. The skill consists of having the attitude and willingness to constantly face complex situations or technical issues, taking the responsibility to guide them towards an effective resolution through whatever path is necessary.

### 3. Creativity and Simplicity

- **Description:** When searching for answers to a problem, it is indispensable to design and select solutions that are immediate, direct, and simple, easing development steps. Concurrently, they must carry a creative element to resourcefully solve the different operations that arise when crafting software.

### 4. Fluid Communication

- **Description:** This plays a highly significant role since a programmer must constantly interact with other people in their environment and work team to achieve a successful final product. Additionally, it is key to conveying the functionality and features of applications to final users, who often know nothing about technology.

### 5. Organization

- **Description:** This is a highly valued trait in programming. It implies being orderly in everyday life and within code development, knowing how to put things in their proper place, and maintaining an optimal distribution of spaces and resources, which guarantees success in project management.

### 6. Basic Math Knowledge

- **Description:** Although mathematics is present throughout the career and within the programming field, it should not be a cause for discouragement. Generally, its practical application is reduced to elementary mathematical logic and basic operations used to build different functions inside a program, so it is not an impediment if one lacks advanced knowledge in the subject.
