# Programming Logic and Methodology

This document compiles the core definitions, methodology, and examples explained in the third episode of the introduction to programming course, translated entirely into English.

## Core Concepts

### Programming

- **Definition:** The act of writing an instruction or a series of instructions using a specific programming language.
- **Programmer:** Someone who creates and delivers precise instructions to a computer so they can be executed later.

### Methodology

- **Definition:** A structured series of steps used systematically to achieve a specific goal or resolve a problem.

---

## Problem-Solving Methodology (4 Essential Steps)

1. **Objective (Problem Analysis):** Extracted by conducting a complete analysis of the problem. It requires reading and analyzing the problem as many times as necessary to fully comprehend it and establish what needs to be solved.
2. **Algorithm Construction:** Creating the detailed plan to reach the objective. An algorithm is a sequential series of steps (which could range from 10, 20, 30, to 100+ steps depending on complexity) designed to find a solution.
3. **Testing:** Evaluating the constructed algorithm by running it through different scenarios to detect errors, find bugs, and identify missing logical paths or improvements.
4. **Presentation:** The final phase where the fully functional solution to the problem is demonstrated and delivered.

---

## Practical Examples

Every program structure fundamentally includes an **Inicio** (Start) and a **Fin** (End) wrapper, which isolates the logical steps of the program.

### Example 1: Acquiring a Book

- **Objective:** Purchase or acquire a specific book.
- **Algorithm Steps:**
  1. Identify which book needs to be acquired.
  2. Travel to a library/bookstore.
  3. Ask the staff if they have the required book available.
  4. **Conditional Branch:**
     - _If available:_ Purchase the book and stop there (reach the end of the program).
     - _If not available:_ Return to step 2 (travel to a different library/bookstore) to repeat the process.

### Example 2: Changing a Flat Car Tire

- **Prerequisites & Tools:** A car with a flat tire, a mechanical jack in good working condition, a spare tire, and a lug wrench.
- **Objective:** Change a flat car tire safely using the provided tools.
- **Algorithm Steps:**
  1. Loosen the lug nuts on the flat tire using the lug wrench.
  2. Position the mechanical jack under the correct lifting point of the vehicle.
  3. Raise the jack until the flat tire can rotate completely free from the ground.
  4. Remove the lug nuts and take off the flat tire.
  5. Mount the spare tire and hand-tighten the lug nuts back into place.
  6. Lower the mechanical jack until the car rests firmly back on the ground and the jack can be freed.
  7. Remove the mechanical jack from underneath the vehicle.
  8. Fully tighten the lug nuts using the lug wrench.
